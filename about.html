The person behind KASIUS.

A founder who saw a broken system and decided to build something that fixes it.

Kevon Martorell

The world doesn't have an information shortage. It has an integrity crisis. Every platform you open is designed to keep you scrolling — not to keep you informed. Algorithms decide what you see, advertisers decide what gets amplified, and the truth gets buried under layers of curation, bias, and noise.

I didn't build KASIUS because I thought the world needed another app. I built it because I was tired of being lied to. Tired of watching critical events get suppressed while outrage bait trends. Tired of trusting systems that were never designed to serve me in the first place.

The Mission

KASIUS was built for people who are tired of fake news and media manipulation. The mission is to give individuals the power to filter through the noise and see what's actually happening — unfiltered, uncurated, and sovereign.

This isn't about building a better feed. It's about building a system that doesn't need a feed at all. One that scans the entire information landscape and surfaces what actually matters — to you, specifically. No middlemen. No editorial board. No algorithm optimizing for engagement over understanding.

KASIUS runs on your hardware, processes on your machine, and answers only to you. That's not a feature. That's the foundation everything else is built on.

Why sovereign intelligence?

Because the moment your intelligence depends on someone else's server, someone else's algorithm, or someone else's business model — it's not yours anymore. Every cloud-based system has a kill switch you don't control, a terms-of-service that can change overnight, and a data pipeline that feeds someone else's model before it feeds yours.

Sovereignty isn't a buzzword. It's the only architecture that guarantees your intelligence system works for you, not against you. KASIUS exists because that guarantee should be the default, not the exception.

Kevon Martorell Founder, KASIUS
